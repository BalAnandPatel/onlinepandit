Nav Durga Puja:Worshiping the Nine Forms of Goddess Durga

Nav Durga Pujais a sacred ritual that reveres thenine divine feminine forcesknown as theNav Durgas. These powerful forms of Goddess Durga play essential roles in the cosmic order—creating, maintaining, and eventually dissolving the universe. Let’s explore the significance, steps, and blessings associated with this puja:

1. Understanding Nav Durga:

TheNav Durgasrepresent different aspects of the divine feminine energy. Each form has unique attributes and symbolism. Here are the nine forms:

  1. Shailputri: The daughter of the mountains, symbolizing strength and purity.
  2. Brahmacharini: The ascetic form, representing devotion and penance.
  3. Chandraghanta: The warrior goddess with a crescent moon on her forehead.
  4. Kushmanda: The creator of the universe, radiating cosmic energy.
  5. Skandmata: The mother of Lord Kartikeya, signifying motherly love.
  6. Katyayani: The fierce form, born to defeat the demon Mahishasura.
  7. Kalratri: The dark and powerful goddess, destroyer of negativity.
  8. Mahagauri: The pure and compassionate form.
  9. Siddhidatri: The bestower of spiritual and material achievements.

2. Nav Durga Puja Steps:

  1. Dhyana and Avahana (Invocation):

    • Begin by meditating on Goddess Durga and invoking her presence.
    • Chant the following mantra while showing the Avahan Mudra (joining both palms and folding thumbs inward):
      • “Sarvamangala Mangalye Shive Sarvartha Sadhike…”

  2. Asana (Offering a Seat):

    • Offer a seat to Goddess Durga by placing five flowers in front of her image.
    • Chant the mantra:
      • “Aneka Ratnasamyuktam Nanamanigananvitam…”

  3. Padya Prakshalana (Foot-Washing):

    • Offer water to wash the feet of Goddess Durga.
    • Chant the mantra:
      • “Gangadi Sarvatirthebhyo Maya Prarthanayahritam…”

  4. Arghya Samarpan (Offering Scented Water):

    • Offer scented water to Goddess Durga.
    • Chant the mantra:
      • “Gandhapushpakshatairyuktamarghyam Sampaditam Maya…”

  5. Achamana Samarpan (Water for Sipping):

    • Offer water for sipping (achamana) to Goddess Durga.
    • Chant the mantra:
      • “Achamyatam Tvaya Devi Bhakti Me Hyachalam Kuru…”

  6. Snana (Bathing):

    • Offer water for the bath (snana) to Goddess Durga.
    • Chant the mantra:
      • “Payodadhi Ghritam Kshiram Sitaya Cha Samanvitam…”

  7. Vastra (Clothing):

    • Offer new clothes (moli) to Goddess Durga.
    • Chant the mantra:
      • “Vastram Cha Soma Daivatyam Lajjayastu Nivaranam…”

  8. Abhushana Samarpan (Jewelry Offering):

    • Offer jewelry to Goddess Durga.
    • Chant the mantra:
      • “Hara Kankana Keyura Mekhala Kundaladibhih…”

3. Benefits of Nav Durga Puja:

  • Spiritual growth and well-being.
  • Positive impact on personal and professional life.
  • Creation of a harmonious environment.
