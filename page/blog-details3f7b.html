Ganesh Pooja

Ganesh Pooja, also known as Ganesh Chaturthi or Vinayaka Chaturthi, is a Hindu festival celebrated in honor of Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles and the god of wisdom and prosperity.

The festival usually lasts for 10 days, with the main festivities taking place on the fourth day of the waxing moon period in the Hindu month of Bhadrapada (usually August or September).

During Ganesh Pooja, devotees install clay idols of Lord Ganesha in their homes or community pandals (temporary structures). They perform elaborate rituals including prayers, offerings of flowers, sweets, and coconuts, and singing devotional songs in praise of Lord Ganesha.

Ganesh Pooja is celebrated with great enthusiasm and fervor across India and in many parts of the world. It is a time for family gatherings, community events, and spreading joy and happiness.

If you wish to organize a Ganesh Pooja at your home or community, or learn more about the significance of the festival, please feel free to contact us. Our experienced priests will guide you through the rituals and help make your celebration a memorable one.
